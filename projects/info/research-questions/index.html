Research Questions!

Here are some anticipated questions that I have endeavoured to answer!

What is hypertension?

Hypertension is characterized as a long-term period of dangerously elevated blood pressure levels that may cause heart disease. There are some different types of hypertension. Malignant hypertension is hypertension that develops suddenly and extremely, secondary hypertension is hypertension caused by another disease, and renal hypertension is hypertension caused by kidney disease.

Why is hypertension dangerous?

Hypertension can cause a variety of ailments, including atherosclerosis, which contributes to coronary artery disease, kidney disease/failure, strokes, hypertensive retinopathy (eye damage), diabetes, various heart diseases, and more.

What are some of the causes of hypertension?

Many things can cause hypertension, including stress, alcohol abuse, drug abuse, lack of exercise, injury, tobacco use, high sodium, sleep loss, and other disorders.

Who is most likely to suffer from hypertension?

Hypertension affects black people more often than white people, old people more often than young people, and men more often than women at younger ages, but women more often than men at older ages. Geography also plays a role, with a “stroke belt” of 10 states in SE USA with a higher likelihood of death by stroke.

How can hypertension be alleviated or controlled?

There are a lot of ways to help alleviate or control hypertension. The primary one that comes to most people’s minds is a low sodium diet. Others include streducing stress and engaging in more cardiovascular exerise.
